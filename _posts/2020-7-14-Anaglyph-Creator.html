First, what is an anaglyph?

An anaglyph is a stereoscopic image, or composed of two images taken slightly apart from each other (like how your eyes see!), in which both images are superimposed and are two different colors (usually cyan and red). If you look at this image through colored filters (like 3D glasses), then the image will 'pop out' of the screen.

Anaglyphs have been used since the 1850's, starting with Joseph D'Almeida who invented a methodology for showing stereoscopic images to multiple people at once. They have been used to create 3D images and even for some early 3D movies. You can read more on the history of anaglyphs here.

How was this done?

To complete this project, I used OpenCV and numpy libraries. I read in my left and right images using OpenCV and then created a slightly larger empty image array using numpy to store the two overlapping images as a final anaglyph. I used an offset of 5 pixels for the images above.
I then iterate through the two images, taking the blue and green channel from the left and the red channel from the right, saving the red pixels as normal and then offsetting the blue and green pixels by an offset amount. I then save and preview the image.
